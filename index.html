SleepLM: Natural-Language Intelligence for Human Sleep

Zongzhe Xu1, Zitao Shuai1, Eideen Mozaffari1, Ravi Shankar Aysola1, Rajesh Kumar1, Yuzhe Yang1
1University of California, Los Angeles
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

SleepLM is, to our knowledge, the first sleep-language foundation model family for targeted PSG→text generation and sleep↔text retrieval, trained on a large paired sleep–text corpus from NSRR cohorts.

Abstract

We present SleepLM, a family of sleep-language foundation models that align multimodal polysomnography (PSG) with natural language to enable open-vocabulary sleep understanding, retrieval, and natural-language reporting. In contrast to prior learning-based sleep systems that operate in closed label spaces (e.g., predefined stages or events), SleepLM supports free-form, clinically meaningful descriptions grounded in physiology.

To support scalable sleep–text alignment, we curate a multilevel captioning pipeline that produces channel-specific, local temporal, and global epoch-level captions, yielding a large paired sleep–text corpus from five NSRR cohorts (100K+ hours of PSG; 10K+ individuals). We further introduce ReCoCa, a unified pretraining objective that combines contrastive alignment, caption generation, and signal reconstruction to preserve physiological fidelity while learning strong cross-modal semantics.

SleepLM enables controllable / targeted generation (brain, cardiac, respiration, somatic) and supports sleep↔text retrieval, zero-/few-shot generalization, and robust transfer to unseen concepts.

SleepLM is a research prototype and is not clinically validated for diagnosis or medical decision-making.

Motivation

Human sleep is expressed through dense, continuous physiology, yet most computational sleep pipelines reduce it to a small set of labels. This makes it difficult to describe nuanced phenomena, query recordings with natural language, or generalize beyond predefined taxonomies.

SleepLM aims to bridge this gap by learning a mapping between PSG and language at scale, enabling interactive and open-ended sleep analysis: “find epochs with irregular breathing and arousals”, “summarize respiration over the night”, or “describe EEG patterns consistent with N2”.

Model Architecture: ReCoCa

SleepLM is built on the Reconstructive Contrastive Captioner (ReCoCa) framework to jointly learn (i) a shared embedding space for sleep↔text alignment and (ii) a conditional captioner for physiologically grounded generation.

  • Channel-aware sleep encoder: encodes multi-sensor PSG with explicit channel structure to capture modality-specific morphology and cross-channel interactions.
  • Text decoder for targeted generation: generates natural-language descriptions conditioned on the encoded PSG and optional domain prompts (brain / cardiac / respiration / somatic) for controllable reporting.
  • Unified ReCoCa objective: combines contrastive alignment (for retrieval), captioning (for generation), and reconstruction (for physiological fidelity).

See the paper for architectural details and training protocol.

Capabilities

SleepLM supports a set of language-first capabilities for sleep analysis:

  • Targeted (controllable) captioning: generate domain-specific descriptions for brain, cardiac, respiration, or somatic physiology, as well as holistic summaries.
  • Cross-modal retrieval: retrieve relevant sleep segments from text queries (text→sleep) and retrieve descriptive text from sleep segments (sleep→text).
  • Open-vocabulary generalization: transfer to unseen concepts and tasks beyond fixed label spaces, including zero-shot and few-shot settings.
  • Physiology-aware representations: embeddings that remain grounded in signal structure via reconstructive pretraining.

Results

We evaluate SleepLM across a broad benchmark spanning zero-shot recognition, cross-modal retrieval, and data-efficient adaptation. The full set of quantitative results, ablations, and qualitative examples are provided in the paper.
